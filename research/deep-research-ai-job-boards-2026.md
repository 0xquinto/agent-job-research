## Executive Summary

The AI job market in 2026 is large, fragmented, and increasingly served by niche boards rather than general aggregators. AI roles are approximately 3x more likely to be remote than average tech roles. AI-related job postings grew ~65% year-over-year in 2025, with remote/hybrid representing ~53% of newly created AI positions. Demand consistently outpaces supply for LLM Engineers, MLOps/LLMOps Engineers, AI Agent Builders, and AI Solutions Architects. For roles like MCP developer, multi-agent orchestration, context engineering, and inference engineering — these are emerging titles that appear mainly on general tech boards (LinkedIn, Wellfound) and in Hacker News "Who's Hiring" threads, not yet on dedicated niche boards.

## Tier 1: Primary Boards (Highest ROI for Diego's Profile)

### 1. LinkedIn Jobs
- **URL:** https://linkedin.com/jobs
- **Best for:** Mid-to-senior AI engineering, DevRel, AI Ops, LLMOps — all role types Diego is targeting
- **Quality:** High for discovery; 87% of recruiters use LinkedIn as primary sourcing tool (Jobvite 2024)
- **Volume:** Massive; largest professional network with 1B+ members
- **Remote-friendliness:** Strong remote filter; verify actual policy on company career page
- **International support:** Good; profile-based discovery helps international candidates bypass ATS language issues
- **Conversion notes:** Easy Apply creates high-volume applicant pools (300+ applicants common). Best used as networking platform + job board combo. Combine applications with direct recruiter DMs. Many roles filled before public posting via LinkedIn Recruiter.
- **For Diego specifically:** Publish content demonstrating AI agent / MCP expertise to attract inbound. Featured section with 2-4 project case studies (RAG demo, agent workflow, evaluation harness) critical.
- **Strategy:** Apply via company career page after finding role on LinkedIn. Supplement with direct outreach to hiring manager.

### 2. Wellfound (formerly AngelList Talent)
- **URL:** https://wellfound.com
- **Best for:** Startup AI roles (seed to Series C+), AI infrastructure, AI agent companies, LLM startups
- **Quality:** 9/10 for startup-exclusive listings — 130,000+ active jobs, all direct from employers (no staffing agencies)
- **Volume:** 35,000+ companies, 10M+ registered candidates
- **Remote-friendliness:** Strong; salary, equity, and remote terms shown upfront before applying
- **International support:** Decent; VPN usage can trigger account bans (disable VPN when creating account if using one)
- **Conversion notes (critical):** Scale.jobs 2026 analysis found ~80% of applications expire without employer review; only 5-6% of applicants reach interview. Root cause: frictionless one-click apply floods employer queues. Best used as a research/discovery tool, not a primary apply channel. Application tracker shows "viewed/expired" status which is useful intelligence.
- **Salary filter flaw:** Enabling salary filter removes most listings (startups don't post comp publicly). Cross-reference any listing against Glassdoor filtered for <50 employee companies.
- **No mobile app** as of March 2026 (Android app removed March 2024, no iOS app).
- **For Diego specifically:** Use to identify which AI agent / LLM / MCP-focused startups are actively hiring. Then apply directly to their career page or cold-email the hiring manager. Wellfound's company stage filters (seed, Series A, B+) are excellent for targeting the right-sized companies.
- **Ghost job warning:** Cross-check on Crunchbase — if last funding round was 18+ months ago and headcount is flat, listing is likely ambient pipeline-building. Listings older than 60 days with vague stack requirements are almost certainly stale.

### 3. Hacker News "Who is Hiring?" (Monthly Thread)
- **URL:** https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47219668 (March 2026 thread)
- **Best for:** Engineering-first startups, YC-backed companies, AI agent/infra roles, MCP developer roles, context engineering roles
- **Quality:** Very high — founders/engineers post directly, no recruiters, no spam. March 2026 thread active.
- **Volume:** Lower than mainstream boards but extremely high signal
- **Remote-friendliness:** Must say "REMOTE" explicitly; many listings include remote-only or REMOTE (US only) — check carefully
- **International support:** Variable; some roles restrict to US, EU or specific countries. Always check before investing time.
- **Conversion notes:** Best conversion rate of any channel for technical roles. Founders who post personally often respond to direct email.
- **For Diego specifically:** This is where MCP developer, multi-agent orchestration, context engineering, and inference engineering roles appear before they hit mainstream boards. Search thread with Ctrl+F for "agent", "MCP", "LLM", "context", "inference", "DevRel", "developer advocate".
- **GitHub mirror:** https://nchelluri.github.io/hnjobs/ — searchable version of the thread

## Tier 2: Niche AI/ML Boards (High Relevance, Lower Volume)

### 4. AIMLJobs.fyi
- **URL:** https://www.aimljobs.fyi
- **Best for:** Remote AI engineering roles at AI-first startups
- **Quality:** High curation — focuses on leading AI startups (Anthropic, ElevenLabs, LlamaIndex, Lovable, Vapi, Baseten, etc. verified active on the board as of March 2026)
- **Volume:** Moderate; curated over volume
- **Remote-friendliness:** Remote-first orientation
- **International support:** Moderate; many listings are US-first
- **Notable:** Lists roles with tech stack tags (LangChain, LLM APIs, MLOps, etc.) — useful for filtering to Diego's stack

### 5. AIJobBoard.dev
- **URL:** https://aijobboard.dev
- **Best for:** Prompt engineering, agentic AI, RAG, automation — the exact niche Diego operates in
- **Quality:** Curated niche board; "developer-calm" philosophy (low noise)
- **Volume:** Lower volume — "no jobs found" in some searches (March 2026), suggesting still building critical mass
- **Remote-friendliness:** Remote-first
- **International support:** Worldwide
- **Notable:** Explicitly covers OpenAI, Claude, Gemini, LangChain, LlamaIndex, n8n, Make.com agent frameworks. Best conceptual fit for Diego's profile even if volume is currently low.

### 6. EURemoteJobs.com
- **URL:** https://euremotejobs.com
- **Best for:** EU-accessible remote roles; found "Agentic AI Developer Advocate" and "Context Engineer" roles actively listed (March 2026)
- **Quality:** Moderate; aggregates from multiple sources
- **Volume:** Moderate
- **Remote-friendliness:** Strong EU focus with true remote listings
- **International support:** EU-friendly; good for international candidates

### 7. DailyRemote.com
- **URL:** https://dailyremote.com
- **Best for:** Remote AI/software engineering roles
- **Quality:** Moderate; found "AI Context Engineer" listings (March 2026)
- **Remote-friendliness:** Remote-only focus
- **International support:** Moderate; some US-only restrictions

### 8. RemoteFront.com
- **URL:** https://remotefront.com
- **Best for:** Broad remote job search including LLM roles
- **Quality:** 80,000+ jobs from 6,000 vetted companies, updated hourly — good curation
- **Volume:** High
- **Remote-friendliness:** Core mission
- **International support:** Claimed strong; "25,000+ remote job seekers"
- **Notable:** Has dedicated LLM category (86+ roles found). Lower noise than Indeed for remote AI roles.

- **Remote-friendliness:** Mix; some SF onsite, some remote
- **International support:** Worldwide general application available
- **Notable:** This is the best active board for DevRel roles at AI companies specifically. Diego should monitor this closely.

### 11. General searches for DevRel
- LinkedIn remains the highest-volume source for DevRel roles. Filter by "Developer Advocate" or "Developer Relations" + "AI" or "LLM"
- Wellfound has DevRel listings from startups
- Tech Ladies (hiretechladies.com) had DevRel engineering manager roles (Deepgram example found)

## International Candidate Considerations

- **Diego's situation:** International candidate applying to US/global remote roles
- **Best platforms for international:** Arc.dev (explicit global vetting), Wellfound (with VPN disabled), AIJobBoard.dev (worldwide), RemoteFront, Talents for AI, EURemoteJobs, Contra
- **Avoid or use with caution:** Dice (US-focused), most corporate ATS systems (filter out international resumes via ATS settings — apply directly via company career page)
- **Key insight from trythestack.co (March 2026):** Non-native English speakers face ATS filtering and format parsing issues on mainstream boards. Solution is to prioritize niche boards where technical skills outweigh prose, use reverse recruiting platforms (Arc.dev, Hired), and build a strong GitHub presence
- **Strategy:** 70% specialized/niche platforms, 20% LinkedIn/general, 10% direct company career page applications

## Recommended Search Strategy for Diego

### Priority Stack (ranked by expected ROI)

1. **HN Who's Hiring monthly thread** — search for "agent", "MCP", "context", "inference", "DevRel", "advocate", "ops" every first Monday of month
2. **LinkedIn** — used as networking platform + job board; publish AI agent content to attract inbound; set up alerts for exact title variations
3. **Wellfound** — use as startup discovery tool; build target list of 10-15 AI agent/MCP startups with recent funding (Crunchbase verify); apply directly to their career pages
4. **AIMLJobs.fyi** — monitor for curated AI startup listings
5. **DevRel Talent** — check weekly for DevRel/DevEx roles at AI companies
6. **AIJobBoard.dev** — monitor as it builds volume; perfect conceptual fit
7. **RemoteFront** — secondary sweep for remote LLM/AI roles

### What NOT to do
- Do not spray-apply via Wellfound one-click (80% expire unseen)
- Do not use Indeed as primary channel for AI roles (low signal-to-noise)
- Do not enable Wellfound's salary filter (hides 90% of listings)
- Do not apply to the same role on multiple platforms (duplicate ATS entries)

### Application volume target
8-12 quality applications per week (tailored, with direct recruiter outreach) beats 50+ spray applications. Given Diego's anti-mass-apply philosophy, this aligns exactly with best practices for AI/niche roles.
