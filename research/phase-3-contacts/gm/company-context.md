# Company Context: General Motors (GM)

## Overview
General Motors is a Detroit-based global automaker undergoing a major software and data transformation. The company is investing heavily in AI-driven vehicle experiences, autonomous driving, and data infrastructure to support its software-defined vehicle strategy. Data engineering teams are core to GM's push to monetize connected vehicle data, support marketing analytics, and build real-time data platforms across manufacturing, digital customer, and commercial software domains.

## Recent News (last 3 months — Q1 2026)
- Mar 2026: GM expanding real-world testing of AI-based autonomous driving systems on public roads across select states (expanding beyond simulation/closed-course)
- Mar 2026: GM + LG Energy Solution Tennessee factory bringing back ~700 workers to build batteries for AI data centers — signals data center infrastructure investment
- Nov 2025: GM hired Ed Nightingale (ex-Microsoft VP of Research, ex-Apple Cloud Engineering Director) as VP of Digital Products and Infrastructure — unifying infrastructure and digital products engineering teams; opening Seattle office (~100 engineers)
- Oct 2025: GM announced eyes-off driving for 2028 (Cadillac ESCALADE IQ), conversational AI (Google Gemini) across all vehicles in 2026, and a unified software platform
- Ongoing: GM + NVIDIA partnership on next-gen vehicle AI using NVIDIA DRIVE AGX (Blackwell architecture)
- Ongoing: GM + Google Cloud AI initiatives including Google Gemini integration for in-vehicle conversational AI
- 2026 guidance: GM expects deferred revenue backlog from software/digital services (OnStar ecosystem) to reach $7.5B by end of 2026

## Culture Signals
- Anti-mass-apply friendly: GM recruiters (e.g., Marc Fox) noted to hand-screen all applications — quality over volume
- Engineering culture shifting from automotive OEM toward tech company: hiring from Microsoft, Apple, NVIDIA ecosystem
- Emphasis on mentorship and internal mobility — data engineering managers have moved across multiple domains (manufacturing, analytics, digital)
- Active AWS cloud adoption — engineering managers earning cloud certs signals hands-on technical culture at leadership level
- Strong TIES and community involvement observed in at least one DE manager — suggests values-driven leadership
- Remote-friendly with hybrid expectation (3 days/week if within 50 miles of office; offices in Austin, Detroit/Warren, Atlanta, Mountain View, Milford)

## Team Size & Growth
- GM employs ~76,000 people globally; software/digital engineering headcount growing rapidly
- Data Management & Analytics is a dedicated team/function with multiple active open roles (Staff Data Engineer, Senior Data Engineering Manager, Senior Data Engineer in Motorsports, etc.)
- Multiple parallel hiring tracks: commercial software, applied analytics/insights, digital customer/ecommerce, manufacturing/safety, geospatial/mapping, motorsports — suggests a large, distributed data org
- Senior Data Engineering Manager role in Austin (jr-202508196) signals growth in Texas hub
- Ed Nightingale's hire + Seattle office opening indicates aggressive talent expansion in non-Detroit markets
- CDAO role is currently in flux (Jon Francis left for State Farm ~2025; predecessor Iwao Fusillo left for PepsiCo) — org may be in a rebuilding/reinvention phase at the data leadership level
