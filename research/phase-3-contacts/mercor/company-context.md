# Company Context: Mercor

## Overview

Mercor is a San Francisco-based AI labor marketplace that connects domain experts (lawyers, engineers, doctors, researchers, etc.) with frontier AI labs to generate high-quality training data, evaluations, and RL environments. Founded in 2023 by three college-dropout Thiel Fellows — Brendan Foody (CEO), Adarsh Hiremath (CTO), and Surya Midha (COO, now Board Chairman) — the company has scaled to $500M+ ARR, a $10B valuation, and manages 30,000+ contractors. Clients include OpenAI, Anthropic, Google/Alphabet, and Amazon.

## Recent News

- Feb 2026: Acquired Sepal AI, a data development platform specializing in RL environments and LLM benchmarks; advised by Orrick
- Jan 2026: Released APEX-Agents AI benchmarking report evaluating frontier model performance on business tasks
- Jan 2026: Expanding to New York City
- Oct 2025: Raised $350M Series C at $10B valuation (5x Series B); led by Felicis, with Benchmark and General Catalyst; reached $500M ARR run rate with zero enterprise churn
- May 2025: Hired Sundeep Jain (fmr. Uber CPO) as first President; focus on scaling onboarding, management systems, and customer reporting

## Culture Signals

- Tagline: "For those who care deeply and build boldly"
- Strong in-person collaboration ethos — nearly all full-time roles are onsite SF
- Described by insiders as extremely high-velocity; ex-employee quotes reference "996" schedule intensity
- Kuai Yu (hiring manager): company grew from ~60 to 300+ people in roughly 18 months; prizes "spiky" candidates with deep domain expertise over generalists
- Sid Potdar LinkedIn: team-first culture with strong morale indicators (261 reactions on milestone post, team members commenting positively)
- Controversial public perception: some contractors have reported sudden project cancellations and pay cuts (Forbes, Mar 2026) — worth being aware of for pitch framing

## Tech Stack

- AI/ML infrastructure: custom evaluation pipelines for frontier LLMs

- Modern cloud backend (referenced by CTO Hiremath)

- Job postings reference familiarity with Notion, Airtable, Slack, and standard ops tooling
