# Company Context: Flock Safety

## Overview

Flock Safety is an Atlanta-based public safety technology company offering a full-stack operating system for law enforcement, businesses, and communities — spanning License Plate Readers (LPRs), AI-powered cameras, gunshot detection, and Drone as First Responder (DFR) systems. Trusted by 5,000+ communities and 4,800 law enforcement agencies, the company surpassed $300M ARR in 2025 and raised $275M in February 2026 at a $7.5B valuation. Founded 2017, Y Combinator S17.

## Recent News

- Feb 27, 2026: Raised $275M led by Andreessen Horowitz at $7.5B valuation; total funding $658M. Investment targets U.S. manufacturing expansion, R&D, and product innovation.
- Feb 17, 2026: Workato published feature on Flock Safety's agentic AI strategy, quoting SVP IT Stacey Moore extensively — confirms Workato as their enterprise integration platform.
- Apr 2, 2025: Opened new 97,000-square-foot manufacturing facility in Georgia (announced by Governor Kemp).
- Mar 13, 2025: Earlier $275M funding round announced (reporting varied; appears the Feb 2026 announcement was the final close or a separate round).
- Aug 6, 2025: Published podcast/blog post on product integrations philosophy — "if it doesn't help solve crime, it's not worth building."
- 2025: Named to CNBC Disruptor 50 list.

## Culture Signals

- Anti-bureaucracy, high-autonomy environment typical of late-stage YC companies at pre-IPO scale.
- Public safety mission-driven — employees frequently cite community impact in social posts.
- Heavy investment in AI tooling across all departments; SVP IT has explicit mandate to enable "agentic AI at scale" across revenue and back-office ops.
- IT team is actively building with Workato as orchestration layer — not just maintaining existing systems.
- Leadership encourages IT to lead with "yes" on AI adoption while ensuring governance.
- Fast-growing: 70% YoY revenue growth, 1,400+ employees as of 2025/2026.
- Hiring heavily across IT, engineering, and sales.

## Tech Stack

- **Collaboration/Infra:** Standard SaaS stack inferred for ~1,400-person company
