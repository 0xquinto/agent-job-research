# Negotiation Playbook

## Compensation Target

- Target range: $280,000 - $500,000 USD total compensation
- Minimum acceptable: $200,000 base
- Structure flexibility: open to equity-heavy packages at early-stage companies

---

## Scenario 1: "What are your salary expectations?"

> "Based on market data for this role and level, I'm targeting [RANGE]. I'm flexible
> on structure — base vs equity mix matters less than total package and the opportunity
> to [specific thing about this company]. What range did you have budgeted?"

**Notes:**
- Always redirect to their range after stating yours
- Never give a single number — always a range
- Anchor high within your range

## Scenario 2: Geographic discount pushback

> "The roles I'm competitive for are output-based, not location-based. My portfolio
> — 9-agent orchestration systems, 271-test inference suites, production MCP servers —
> delivers the same value regardless of postal code. I'd want to understand how the
> team thinks about location-adjusted comp."

**Notes:**
- Lead with concrete evidence of output quality
- Don't be combative — frame as a question about their philosophy
- If they insist, ask what the SF-based comp would be as a reference point

## Scenario 3: Offer below target

> "I appreciate the offer. I'm comparing with opportunities in the [higher range].
> I'm drawn to [company] because of [specific reason — team, mission, product].
> Can we explore [target number]? I'm also open to discussing equity or sign-on
> to bridge the gap."

**Notes:**
- Name a competing range, not a competing company (unless strategic)
- Always reaffirm genuine interest before negotiating
- Offer creative structure alternatives (equity, sign-on, review timeline)

## Scenario 4: Downlevel offer

> "I understand. I'd be open to starting at [lower level] if we can agree on:
> (1) a 6-month performance review with clear criteria for promotion, and
> (2) compensation that reflects the higher-level expectations from day one."

**Notes:**
- Don't accept downlevel without a written promotion timeline
- "Clear criteria" = specific deliverables, not vague "impact"
- If comp is also lower, this is two concessions — push back on at least one
